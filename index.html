01

NATO was built for a war
that ended 35 years ago.

NATO was designed in 1949 to stop Soviet tanks from rolling through the Fulda Gap into Western Europe. It was the right alliance for the right threat at the right time.

That threat no longer exists. The Soviet Union collapsed in 1991. Russia — its successor — just spent three years proving it can't defeat Ukraine, a country with a GDP smaller than Florida's. Russian tanks are being destroyed by $500 drones. Their Black Sea fleet was sunk by a country with no navy.

Meanwhile, America pays roughly 70% of NATO's defense costs. Most European members don't hit the 2% GDP spending target they agreed to. Germany spent a decade buying Russian natural gas while expecting America to defend them from Russia. Turkey — a NATO member — bought Russian S-400 missile systems.

NATO isn't an alliance anymore. It's a charity. And America is the only donor.

02

The real fight is in the
Middle East.

The strategic competition that will define the next 50 years isn't about European land wars. It's about energy, the dollar, AI, and China. And all four of those fights run through the Middle East.

China is already moving. Beijing brokered the Saudi-Iran deal in 2023. Chinese trade with Gulf states surged in 2024, making China the region's largest trading partner. Belt and Road investment poured $39 billion into the Middle East that year. The mBridge digital currency platform — designed to bypass the dollar — is processing tens of billions in volume with Gulf state participation.

Every dollar the Gulf invests in Chinese infrastructure is a dollar that doesn't flow through American banks, American Treasuries, or American companies. Every oil barrel settled in yuan is a crack in the petrodollar system that has anchored American economic power since 1974.

China can't offer what America offers — real security guarantees. But China is winning because America hasn't offered anything binding. Gulf states are hedging because they have no choice. Give them a choice.

05

The Abraham Treaty
Organization.

The ATO takes the Abraham Accords — the historic normalization agreements President Trump built between Israel and Arab states — and upgrades them into a full NATO-style defense and economic treaty. Senate-ratified. Permanent. Irreversible.

PILLAR I
Mutual Defense
An Article V-style commitment: an attack on one is an attack on all. The security guarantee Gulf states need to stop hedging toward China. Already proven — Jordan, Saudi Arabia, and Israel jointly intercepted Iranian drones in April 2024.
PILLAR II
American Command
A Supreme Allied Commander Middle East under CENTCOM — always a US four-star general. Just like NATO's SACEUR in Europe. The integrated air defense operations center at Al Udeid is already running. Formalize it.
PILLAR III
The Petrodollar Compact
All member-state oil, defense, and critical mineral trade denominated in US dollars. By treaty. Gulf sovereign wealth funds commit to recycling surpluses into US Treasuries and American infrastructure. mBridge and yuan settlement phased out within 36 months of accession.
PILLAR IV
OPEC as Alliance Asset
OPEC coordination endorsed as compatible with membership — but with an emergency production clause. In a crisis, the alliance can coordinate production releases alongside SPR. OPEC stops being a perceived threat and becomes a strategic tool.
PILLAR V
Technology Lock-In
Members adopt US export controls. No Huawei, no ZTE, no Alibaba Cloud in critical infrastructure. All sovereign AI builds on American hyperscalers — Azure, AWS, Oracle, Google Cloud. China is structurally locked out of the region's tech stack for a generation.
PILLAR VI
The Iran On-Ramp
A dormant accession protocol for a post-theocratic Iran — the Cyrus Accords. Objective criteria for Iranian membership: IAEA compliance, IRGC dissolution, recognition of Israel. Signals to the Iranian people that a path exists. Gives Arab states cover that this isn't anti-Iran — it's conditionally Iran-inclusive.
